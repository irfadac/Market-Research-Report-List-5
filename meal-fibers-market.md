<p><ul><li>Soluble Fiber</li><li>Insoluble Fiber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The meal fibers market is primarily divided into two categories: soluble and insoluble fiber. Soluble fiber dissolves in water, forming a gel-like substance that aids in digestion, regulates blood sugar levels, and lowers cholesterol. It is commonly found in oats, beans, and fruits. Insoluble fiber, on the other hand, does not dissolve in water and adds bulk to the stool, promoting regular bowel movements. Sources include whole grains, nuts, and vegetables. Both types play crucial roles in overall health and wellness.</p></p>
