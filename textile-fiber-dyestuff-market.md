<p><p>The textile fiber dyestuff market comprises various dye types, each suited for different fibers and applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, providing vibrant color with excellent fastness. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fibers, making them ideal for cellulose-based textiles. Sulfur dyes are known for their cost-effectiveness and deep shades on cotton. Vat dyes offer high wash and light fastness, while acid dyes are used mainly for protein and nylon fibers. Other dyes encompass specialty applications and niche markets.</p></p>
