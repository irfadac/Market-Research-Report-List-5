<p><ul><li>Steam Distillation</li><li>Solvent Extraction</li><li>Supercritical Extraction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The onion oil market features three primary extraction methods: steam distillation, solvent extraction, and supercritical extraction. Steam distillation uses steam to separate oil from onion, producing a pure product. Solvent extraction employs organic solvents to dissolve and extract the oil, resulting in higher yield but possibly leaving residual solvents. Supercritical extraction utilizes carbon dioxide at high pressure to extract oil without solvents, preserving flavor and aroma. Each method impacts oil quality, yield, and application in various industries.</p></p>
