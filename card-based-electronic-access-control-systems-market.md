<p><p>Card-based Electronic Access Control Systems are security solutions that utilize electronic card readers to manage entry and exit in various facilities. <p><p>Card-based electronic access control systems can be classified into two main types: networked systems and standalone systems. Networked systems are interconnected and allow centralized management of access control across multiple locations, offering enhanced monitoring and flexibility. They enable real-time updates and integration with other security systems. In contrast, standalone systems operate independently, managing access at a single entry point. They are easier to install and cost-effective for smaller applications but lack the advanced features and scalability of networked systems.</p></p>
