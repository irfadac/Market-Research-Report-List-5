<p><p>The Manifold Absolute Pressure (MAP) sensor market is divided into two primary types: analog sensors and digital sensors. Analog MAP sensors produce a continuous voltage output that varies with pressure changes, providing real-time data to the engine control unit. In contrast, digital MAP sensors deliver discrete signals, often via a serial communication protocol, enhancing integration with modern automotive systems. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing engine performance, fuel efficiency, and emissions control in various vehicle applications.</p></p>
