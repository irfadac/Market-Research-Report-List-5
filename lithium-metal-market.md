<p><p>The lithium metal market primarily consists of two types: lithium sourced from salt lake brine and lithium extracted from mineral ore. Salt lake brine involves extracting lithium from brine pools, typically found in arid regions, where lithium is concentrated over time. In contrast, lithium ore refers to hard rock mining where lithium is obtained from minerals like spodumene. Both methods have distinct supply chains, extraction processes, and environmental impacts, influencing their market dynamics and pricing.</p></p>
