<p><strong>The Peripheral Bone Densitometry Measurement Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Single Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (SEXA)</li><li>Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (pDEXA)</li><li>Radiographic Absorptiometry (RA)</li><li>Quantitative Ultrasound (QUS)</li><li>Peripheral Quantitative Computed Tomography (pQCT)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The peripheral bone densitometry measurement market includes various techniques for assessing bone density. Single Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (SEXA) uses a single X-ray energy level for measurements. Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (pDEXA) utilizes two X-ray energy levels, providing more precise results. Radiographic Absorptiometry (RA) evaluates bone density using standard X-ray images. Quantitative Ultrasound (QUS) employs sound waves for non-ionizing assessments, while Peripheral Quantitative Computed Tomography (pQCT) offers detailed 3D images of bone structure and density. Each method serves different diagnostic needs and patient populations.</p></p>
