<p><p>The Livestock Internal Medicine market can be categorized into two primary segments: external use and internal use. The external use market focuses on treatments applied topically, such as ointments, sprays, and disinfectants, aimed at addressing skin infections, wounds, or parasites. In contrast, the internal use market includes medications administered orally or via injection, targeting systemic diseases, infections, or metabolic disorders. Both segments are crucial for maintaining livestock health, optimizing productivity, and preventing disease outbreaks in agricultural settings.</p></p>
