<p><p>The electrolyte in secondary lithium-ion batteries is crucial for ion transport between the anode and cathode. The liquid electrolyte, often a lithium salt dissolved in organic solvents, is widely used due to its high conductivity and ease of manufacturing. However, it poses safety risks such as leakage and flammability. In contrast, solid electrolytes, composed of solid lithium-ion conductive materials, enhance safety and energy density but face challenges in conductivity and interface stability. Both types are essential for advancing battery technology.</p></p>
