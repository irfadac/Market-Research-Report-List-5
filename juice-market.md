<p><p>Juice markets can be divided into two primary types: concentrate and non-concentrate. Concentrate juice is made by removing water from fresh juice, making it easier to store and transport. It's then reconstituted with water before consumption. Non-concentrate juice, on the other hand, is extracted directly from fresh fruits without any processing that removes water, offering a fresher taste and higher nutritional content. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and market demands, impacting pricing and production practices.</p></p>
