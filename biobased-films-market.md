<p><p>The biobased films market includes several types, prominently featuring PBAT (polybutylene adipate terephthalate) films, PLA (polylactic acid) films, and PHB (polyhydroxybutyrate) films. PBAT films are flexible and biodegradable, making them suitable for packaging applications. PLA films, derived from renewable resources like corn starch, are widely used for food packaging due to their compostability. PHB films are produced from microbial fermentation and offer excellent biodegradability and mechanical properties. Other types encompass various emerging biopolymers aimed at sustainability and eco-friendliness.</p></p>
