<p><p>Music copyright markets are categorized primarily into Royalty Free (RF) and Rights Managed (RM) types. In the RF market, users pay a one-time fee for unlimited usage of tracks without additional royalties, providing affordability and convenience for projects. Conversely, RM music requires licensing fees that vary with the specifics of usage, such as duration, media type, and audience size. This allows for greater control and exclusivity for the rights holders, catering to more specialized and higher-budget projects.</p></p>
